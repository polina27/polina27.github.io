hippocampus

A new way to look back.

As a senior college (and a generally nostalgic person), I love to save and share my memories with friends. So I teamed up with two classmates and created this collaborative virtual scrapbook. hippocampus lets you share pictures, music, text, and more, all on customizable layouts that your friends can also view and edit with you.

hippocampus was the product of my first Hackathon ever, hackCooper. (And it also won 3rd place!)

Outreach in NYC Public Schools

Having role models in the field of STEM is a great way to get and stay inspired.

In 2012 and 2013, I helped Cooper Union receive the Diversity Action Grant from the American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME). With this grant, a group of students and I were able to put together lesson plans and purchase materials with which to lead a hands-on bridge building class for 5th graders from Brooklyn.

@41CooperSquare

Under the advisement of Professor Melody Baglione, @41CooperSquare is my senior design project.

Driven by the goal of working with our building tenants to improve energy use, @41CooperSquare aims to share our building's energy performance with building tenants and visitors alike. By making this information accessible to the public, we hope to encourage green behaviors, inspire support for energy efficiency, and showcase some of the unique features of The Cooper Union.

EBT

One of my passions is bringing together people I love and respect in one place, at one time.

In freshman year, my roommates and I decided that we wanted to celebrate Thanksgiving with all of our new freshman classmates before splitting up and heading home to celebrate with our families. So, we created a potluck event and told our friends to bring in a dish--everything except turkey, that is, because we didn't have functioning ovens in the freshman dorms. We titled this event "Everything But Turkey" (or EBT for short), and due to its overwhelming success, we turned it into a tradition. Every weekend before Thanksgiving, we gathered our best friends and best recipes and just enjoyed each other's company. By senior year, we had a new plan... we all had ovens and it was our last Thanksgiving together with our Cooper family. So, we decided we had to have one last EAT... Everything AND Turkey.
